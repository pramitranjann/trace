# 02 — Problem and HMW

## Problem Statement

Learning to read and write requires repetition, but most educational tools rely heavily on a single learning mode.

Workbooks help learners practice writing, but they are static and silent.
Flashcards help learners recognize symbols, but they do not develop motor memory.
Audio tools help learners hear sounds, but they do not always connect sound to physical production.

As a result, learners can struggle to connect what they see, what they hear, and what they can create themselves.

There is an opportunity to create a more engaging literacy experience that reinforces the same letter or word through multiple forms of repetition: visual recognition, audio recognition, movement, and writing.

## How Might We

How might we help learners build stronger reading and writing confidence by reinforcing the same concepts through seeing, hearing, and producing instead of relying on a single form of repetition?

## Short HMW

How might we make letter repetition feel playful, physical, and memorable?

## Design Challenge

Move beyond the textbook without abandoning repetition.

The product should still support repeated practice, but the repetition should feel interactive, audio-based, camera-based, physical, and rewarding.
